AIDE Workshop

Agentic Intelligence for Discovery and Exploration. A collaborative workshop that helps faculty and student research teams explore how agentic AI workflows can accelerate scientific discovery. Participants bring their own research problem and work alongside AI-focused graduate student collaborators to implement and evaluate agentic approaches on real research challenges.

Background

Why Agentic Workflows?

Agentic workflows are emerging as a new paradigm for scientific discovery, in which AI systems do not merely answer questions or assist with isolated tasks, but actively participate in the research loop: proposing hypotheses, generating candidate solutions, writing and modifying code, running experiments or evaluations, learning from feedback, and iteratively improving their outputs.

Recent systems such as AlphaEvolve have shown that large language models, when coupled with evolutionary search and rigorous evaluators, can discover new algorithms and optimizations that improve on existing state-of-the-art solutions in mathematics, computer science, and large-scale computing systems. More recent frameworks such as SkyDiscover extend this idea by making the discovery loop modular and open, enabling researchers to plug in their own problems, evaluators, search strategies, and domain-specific constraints.

Together, these developments suggest that agentic scientific workflows may become a powerful new research methodology: one in which faculty and student teams bring deep domain expertise, while AI agents help explore large design spaces, automate experimentation, and uncover promising directions that may lead to new results and publications.

Format

How It Works

Faculty bring their own research problem and may bring one or two graduate students from their research group to form a research team. We will identify a few graduate students who will act as consultants and serious collaborators to help each research team run their problem with the agentic workflow to see if it can improve the state of the art. Hopefully, that leads to new results and publications.

Through daily tutorials, implementation support, research sprints, and community discussions, AIDE provides the tools, infrastructure, and expertise needed to rapidly prototype agentic research workflows. The goal is simple: determine whether these emerging methods can help generate new insights, improve research productivity, and ultimately lead to publishable scientific advances.

Logistics & Access

What We Provide

We want the barrier to entry to be as low as possible. You bring the domain expertise and research problem — we handle the rest.

API Access

We set up API access and infrastructure so you can start experimenting with agentic workflows from day one — no setup headaches.

Expert Guidance

Dedicated AI-focused graduate student collaborators work closely with your team throughout the week, not just during office hours.

Follow-up Sessions

Promising research threads can continue beyond the workshop with follow-up sessions planned for the Fall semester.

Path to Publication

The workshop is structured around real research goals — new results, improved baselines, and publishable scientific advances.
